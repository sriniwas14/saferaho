# Own Damage vs Third-Party Motor Insurance

Motor insurance is easier to compare when you separate legal compliance from practical protection. Third-party cover handles your legal requirement. Own damage cover protects your vehicle.

## What third-party cover does

Third-party insurance covers damage or injury caused by your vehicle to another person or property. It helps you stay compliant, but it does not pay for repairs to your own car.

## What own damage cover adds

Own damage insurance covers losses to your vehicle due to accidents, natural calamities, fire, and theft, subject to policy terms.

## Why add-ons matter

Zero depreciation, roadside assistance, engine protection, and return to invoice can materially change claim value. These are not mandatory, but they can be worth it depending on your vehicle and usage.

## Do not ignore IDV

The Insured Declared Value affects both premium and claim payout in total loss scenarios. Picking the lowest value only to reduce premium can backfire later.

## Conclusion

A good motor policy is not just valid. It is usable when repair costs, downtime, and claim friction hit at the same time.
