# International Travel Insurance Checklist

Travel insurance is easiest to undervalue before a trip and easiest to appreciate during one. The right policy protects more than medical emergencies.

## Confirm the medical sum insured

Medical coverage is usually the headline feature for international trips. Make sure the sum insured matches the destination risk and visa requirements where applicable.

## Check trip interruption and delay benefits

Flight disruptions can create hotel, transfer, and rebooking costs very quickly. Review delay triggers and payout conditions instead of assuming all disruptions are covered.

## Review baggage and passport support

Lost baggage and lost passport benefits matter most when you understand the claim documents required. Convenience during travel often depends on those support features.

## Look at exclusions for adventure and pre-existing conditions

Adventure activities, specific sports, and pre-existing medical issues may be excluded or limited. This is where many travelers discover the policy they bought is narrower than expected.

## Conclusion

Buy travel insurance with your actual itinerary in mind. Destination, trip duration, age, and activities should drive the plan choice, not just the lowest premium.
