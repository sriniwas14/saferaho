# Cashless Health Insurance: What Actually Matters Before You Buy

![Getting Started with Health Insurance](/images/health_insurance/getting_started.webp)

Cashless health insurance sounds simple: choose a hospital in the insurer's network, get treated, and let the insurer settle the bill directly. In practice, the details around limits and exclusions decide whether the experience is smooth or frustrating.

## Check the hospital network first

Do not start with premium alone. Start with the hospitals you would realistically use in your city. A long network list is less useful than having the right hospitals close to you.

## Understand room rent limits

Many plans look attractive until you notice room category restrictions. A room rent cap can increase your out-of-pocket spend because several hospital charges are linked to the room type you choose.

## Review waiting periods carefully

Waiting periods affect when you can claim for specific illnesses, pre-existing diseases, and maternity-related expenses. A lower premium today is not always worth a stricter waiting period later.

## Restoration and no-claim bonus are not the same

Restoration benefits refill the sum insured after usage under specific conditions. A no-claim bonus increases your cover when you do not claim. Both are useful, but they solve different problems.

## Conclusion

Choose a plan only after checking network quality, room rent terms, waiting periods, and restoration structure together. That is the difference between buying cover and buying usable cover.
