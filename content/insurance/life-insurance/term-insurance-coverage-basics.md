# How Much Term Insurance Cover Is Enough?

Term insurance is not about choosing a large round number. The right cover should replace income, clear liabilities, and protect major family goals if you are no longer around.

## Start with income replacement

A common mistake is comparing premiums before deciding the coverage need. Begin with the number of years your family would need support and estimate the capital required to replace that income.

## Add loans and major liabilities

Home loans, personal loans, and business debt should be added separately. Otherwise, your family may inherit financial pressure exactly when stability matters most.

## Include future goals

Children's education, marriage, and retirement support for a spouse often require a meaningful addition beyond basic debt coverage.

## Avoid mixing investment with protection

For pure income protection, term plans are usually the cleaner structure. Keep the insurance decision focused on protection, then plan investments separately.

## Conclusion

An adequate cover amount is one that protects your family standard of living, not one that merely feels affordable on the premium screen.
