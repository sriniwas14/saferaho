# Top Tax Saving Investment Options for 2026

Tax planning is an essential part of financial management. By choosing the right investment options, you can reduce your tax liability while building wealth.

## Section 80C Investments (Up to ₹1.5 Lakh Deduction)

### 1. ELSS Funds
Equity Linked Savings Scheme offers tax benefits with the shortest lock-in period of 3 years. Historically, ELSS has delivered higher returns compared to traditional tax-saving options.

### 2. PPF (Public Provident Fund)
- **Lock-in**: 15 years
- **Returns**: 7-8% (tax-free)
- **Risk**: Very low (government-backed)

### 3. EPF (Employee Provident Fund)
- **Contribution**: 12% of basic salary
- **Returns**: 8-9% (tax-free at maturity)
- **Best for**: Salaried employees

### 4. NSC (National Savings Certificate)
- **Lock-in**: 5 years
- **Returns**: 7-8%
- **Risk**: Very low

## Section 80D: Health Insurance Premium

Deduction up to ₹25,000 for self and family (₹50,000 for senior citizens). Additional ₹25,000 for parents (₹50,000 if senior citizens).

## Section 80CCD(1B): NPS

Additional deduction of ₹50,000 for investments in National Pension System.

## Comparison Table

| Investment | Lock-in | Returns | Risk | Tax on Returns |
|------------|---------|---------|------|----------------|
| ELSS | 3 years | 12-15% | High | Tax-free (LTCG up to ₹1L) |
| PPF | 15 years | 7-8% | Very Low | Fully Tax-free |
| NPS | Till 60 years | 8-10% | Medium | Partially Taxable |
| NSC | 5 years | 7-8% | Very Low | Taxable |

## Tips for Tax Planning

1. **Start early** in the financial year
2. **Diversify** across different tax-saving instruments
3. **Consider liquidity needs** before locking funds
4. **Review annually** and rebalance if needed
